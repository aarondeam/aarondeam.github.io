Issues



The American founders wanted the House of Representatives to be responsive to the people in their local settings. They wanted the widely varying local perspectives of the American people to affect the work of Congress and its weighing of national priorities and policy choices.


To be faithful to its Constitutional design, anyone elected to the U.S. House of Representatives has an obligation to put the people of their district first, ahead of party and personal ideology, and to work with respect and diligence with other members of Congress representing the people of their districts and states.


A Better Politics


Dirk believes that a better politics depends on a renewed commitment to representing all people of the district and working hard to find compromises among their varying interests and needs. This requires speaking and listening to constituents of all political persuasions— something Dirk has had decades of practice doing. Too often politics is dismissed for partisan gridlock or special interest corruption— but Dirk believes in both the power of national government to solve problems that affect us all and the precision of local government to identify Iowans’ specific concerns. As Congressman, he will work hard to bridge connections between the two and better represent the people of Iowa.


Healthcare and Retirement Security


Dirk believes that government should do its best to help its citizens attain dependable, affordable, and widespread medical coverage. A healthy democracy depends on a healthy population. Healthcare, Social Security, and Medicare should therefore be seen as a way to strengthen the society of America, by connecting generations, doctors, and patients—a social web rather than a safety net.


Education


Both Dirk and his wife Annie have dedicated their lives to public education in Iowa. They realize the fundamental importance of public schools as the bedrock of communities. Dirk views the classroom as a model democratic space, where common sense and courtesy rule, and students are encouraged to voice their questions and concerns. As the wellspring of a functioning democracy, classrooms therefore require adequate support from the public institutions that depend on them.


Energy, Infrastructure, and the Environment


Dirk believes in the power of 21st century infrastructure revitalization as a source of national commitment and pride. Better highways, roads, and facilities, will bring jobs, prosperity, and a cleaner environment to Iowa. Our soil, fields, and rivers are the assets upon which found our common future. With a better balance of local and global considerations for climate change and energy production, Dirk believes that a more efficient, productive, and commonly celebrated infrastructure and environment is possible.
